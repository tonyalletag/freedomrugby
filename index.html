Respect is at the heart of rugby.
All of the fans know it and all of the players demonstrate it. Because respect is one of the defining characteristics of rugby, more and more people are drawn to the sport each year.The collective respect for the game forms a sense of brotherhood, sisterhood, and camaraderie shared between players in a club and the mutual respect for all rugby players.

After a match the home team organizes a social get-together after the game, one in which both teams hang out and socialize. Rugby's culture features a noble and honorable combination of camaraderie, loyalty and respect that’s not found in other sports. Fans know it to be unique to the sport of rugby; players recognize it as something that, compared to other athletes, makes them a cut above the rest.

How the game scheduling works.
The coaches touch base on Monday to see how many sides they will have in each age bracket. It varies depending on who is on vacation and who is here. Once that is determined, the home team will set the schedule. So by Thursday or Friday of each week, you should know what time your child is playing on Saturday. It does change weekly but it will always be in the morning and we should all be done by noonish!

You don't want to miss this event
At the end of every season all the rugby teams in the league gather at one location to have a huge rugby tournament. This is an all day affair! We will be at the fields around 8am and we do not usually end until around 3PM. The kids will have 4 or 5 games on this day. The way the tournament works is on Saturday the U9 and U11 will play all day and on Sunday the U13s and U15s will play.

FAQ'S

(Q) Is youth Rugby a non-contact sport?
(A) Youth Rugby is a minimal contact sport. Players with the ball are tagged by the opposing team and there is no tackling in youth rugby. It can be described as a big game of tag, and whenever you get a bunch of kids running and moving quickly on a field there may be some incidental contact.

(Q) How often are practices, and during what times?
(A) Every Tuesday and Thursday at 6:30pm-8pm starting the 2nd week of May. Practices are at Hodges park in Sykesville.

(Q) What equipment will I need to purchase for my child?
(A) Cleats and a water bottle, that is it.

(Q) How long does the season run?
(A) Practices start the 2nd week of May and games go through the end of July

(Q) What are the age divisions?
(A) The teams are grouped into the following U9, U11, U13, U15 Cut off dates for Rugby ages are Sept 1st. If your child turns 9 after Sept 1st they will be in the U9’s, If your child turns 9 before Sept 1st they will be in U11.

(Q) Where are the games played?
(A) Home games are played at Freedom Park in Sykesville. Away games are local to the area depending on who we are playing.
